Gameplay

Shapes called Tetrominoes fall from the top of the playfield to the bottom. Tetrominoes consist of four blocks arranged into seven different patterns.
The object of the game is to manipulate these Tetrominoes with the aim of creating a horizontal line of blocks without gaps. When such a line is created, it disappears, and the blocks above (if any) fall.
When the player clears 10 lines the level increases and the Tetrominoes fall faster.
The game ends when the player "tops out", that is, when the stack of tetrominoes reaches the top of the playing field and no new Tetrominoes are able to enter.

Game Modes
1 Player – Standard Jetris game
2 Player – Three different modes:
  1. First to Lose (FTL) – When either player fills their game panel to the top, their opponent will be declared the winner.
  2. First to get X Amount of Lines (FXL) – When either player removes a certain number of lines, that player will be declared the winner.
  3. First to a Certain Score (FTS) – When either player acquires a certain score, that player will be declared the winner.
Special Blocks

When one player clears four lines at once, the user will be helped by a special event, or the opposing player will be attacked with a special event.  A special event block will appear on the screen – Good blocks will appear on the player’s screen and bad blocks will appear on the opposing player’s screen.  When the player clears a line with a special event block, the special block’s corresponding event will occur.

Good Blocks
Destroyer Block: The player that cleared the lines will have their screen cleared of all blocks.
Bomb Block: The player that cleared the lines will have the blocks (3x3 area) around the Bomb Block cleared from their screen.
Decrease Speed: The player that cleared the lines will have the speed of their game decreased by 50 ms.

Bad Blocks
Add 4 Block: The opposing player will get four lines added to the bottom.
Four Corner Block: The opposing player’s next piece will be a Four Corner Block.
Staircase Block: The opposing player’s next piece will be a Staircase Block.
Increase Speed: The opposing player will have the speed of their game increased by 50 ms.
Key Controls
  • A or Left Arrow - Move the Tetromino to left
  • D or Right Arrow - Move the Tetromino to right
  • W or Up Arrow - Rotate the Tetromino
  • S or Down Arrow - Move the Tetromino quickly down
  • Space - Drop the Tetromino immediately
  • P or 'Pause' Button - Pause
  • R or 'Restart' Button - Restart
  • H - View HiScore
  • Esc - Exit Jetris

Key Configuration
The Key Configuration menu allows the users to change the controls for the gameplay of Jetris.  The Key Configuration menu can be accessed through the 'K' hot key, or by navigating to Jetris>KeyConfig.

The following controls can be changed in the Key Configuration Menu:
Rotate
Move Left
Move Right
Move Down
Drop

If in 1 or 2 player mode, click in the field of the control you wish to change and press the key on the keyboard that you wish to be the new key for the corresponding control.

Press OK, to save the changes
Press Cancel, to cancel the changes and continue with the previous key configuration
Press Default, to return to the default values
Scoring System
  • Clearing 1 Line at once, gives You 100 points + 5 x the current level
  • Clearing 2 Line at once, gives You 400 points + 20 x the current level
  • Clearing 3 Line at once, gives You 900 points + 45 x the current level
  • Clearing 4 Line at once, gives You 1600 points + 80 x the current level
For example:
The current level is 20 (the highest level) and You clear 4 Lines at once, then You get 1600 + 80 x 20 = 2 x 1600 = 3200. So on level 20 you are making twice as much points as on level 0.
